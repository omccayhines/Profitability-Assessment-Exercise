Profitability Assessment: Which Products Drive Our Success?

A Hands-On Analytics Exercise

We operate 20 products across the Magical Marketplace generating $381,755 in revenue. Our task: identify which products are truly profitable and deserve our strategic focus.

The Challenge

lightbulb What You Need to Do

You'll analyze the Magical Marketplace product portfolio to determine which products are truly driving profitability. This isn't just about revenue—it's about understanding the complete financial picture.

Why This Matters: Revenue tells only part of the story. A high-revenue product with equally high costs may be less valuable than a moderate-revenue product with excellent margins. Your analysis will reveal which products deserve strategic investment and which may need reconsideration.

High-Level Business Steps

1
Define Your Business Question
expand_more

Primary Question: Which products in our Magical Marketplace portfolio are most profitable?

Sub-Questions to Consider:

  • Which products generate the highest total profit?
  • Which products have the best profit margins?
  • Are high-volume products also high-profit products?
  • Which products underperform and why?

Clear business questions guide your entire analysis and ensure actionable insights.

2
Gather the Right Data
expand_more

Required Data Sources:

  • Product Master Data: Product names, categories, and identifiers
  • Cost Data: Unit cost for each product (COGS)
  • Sales Transactions: Sales price, quantity sold, transaction dates
  • Currency Information: Exchange rates if dealing with multiple currencies

Data Quality Checks: Verify completeness, check for duplicates, validate currency conversions, and ensure consistent product identifiers across datasets.

3
Calculate Revenue First
expand_more

Formula: Revenue = Sales Price × Quantity Sold

Implementation in Power Query:

  • Create a custom column for revenue calculation
  • Ensure consistent currency (convert if necessary)
  • Aggregate at the product level (sum of all transactions)

Why Start Here: Revenue provides the baseline for understanding product performance and is essential for calculating profitability metrics.

4
Calculate Cost of Goods Sold (COGS)
expand_more

Formula: COGS = Unit Cost × Quantity Sold

Key Considerations:

  • Merge product cost data with sales transactions
  • Ensure unit costs are in the same currency as sales prices
  • Calculate total COGS per product across all transactions

Common Pitfall: Forgetting to account for volume—a low unit cost can still result in high total COGS if sales volume is substantial.

5
Calculate Gross Profit
expand_more

Formula: Gross Profit = Revenue - COGS

What This Tells You:

  • Absolute profitability of each product
  • Which products contribute most to bottom line
  • Total profit contribution across the portfolio

Analysis Tip: Sort products by gross profit to identify your top performers. These are your profit leaders that deserve strategic focus.

6
Calculate Profit Margin
expand_more

Formula: Profit Margin (%) = (Gross Profit ÷ Revenue) × 100

Why Margins Matter:

  • Reveals efficiency independent of scale
  • Identifies products with best return on sales
  • Enables comparison across different product sizes

Strategic Insight: A product with lower absolute profit but higher margin may be more strategically valuable, especially if it has growth potential.

7
Analyze & Interpret
expand_more

Key Analysis Questions:

  • Which products are both high-volume AND high-margin? (Stars)
  • Which products have high volume but low margins? (Cash cows or problem products)
  • Which products have high margins but low volume? (Growth opportunities)
  • Which products are underperforming on both dimensions? (Candidates for discontinuation)

Deliverable: Create a clear summary table and visualization showing product performance across all key metrics.

code

Power Query Quick Reference

Power Query Implementation Steps
expand_more

1. Load Your Data Tables:

  • Import Sales Transactions table
  • Import Product Master table (with costs)
  • Import Currency Exchange table (if needed)

2. Key Merge Operations:

  • Merge Sales with Products: Left join on Product ID to bring in cost data
  • Merge with Currency: If needed, join on currency code to get exchange rates

3. Calculated Columns to Create:

  • Revenue: [Sales Price] × [Quantity]
  • COGS: [Unit Cost] × [Quantity]
  • Gross Profit: [Revenue] - [COGS]
  • Profit Margin: ([Gross Profit] / [Revenue]) × 100

4. Aggregation Approach:

  • Group by Product Name/ID
  • Sum: Revenue, COGS, Gross Profit
  • Count: Number of transactions
  • Calculate: Average profit margin

Common Pitfalls

Pitfall Why It Happens How to Avoid
Currency Conversion Mistakes Mixing currencies without conversion or using wrong exchange rates Standardize all values to one currency early; verify exchange rates are current
Revenue vs. Profit Confusion Focusing only on revenue without considering costs Always calculate and compare both metrics; remember high revenue ≠ high profit
Transaction vs. Product Level Analyzing individual transactions instead of aggregating by product Use Group By in Power Query to aggregate at product level
Ignoring Volume Looking at unit metrics without considering total volume impact Analyze both per-unit margins AND total profit contribution
Missing Data Joins Incomplete merges leaving products without cost data Check for null values after merges; investigate unmatched records
